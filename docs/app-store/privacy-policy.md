# Varq Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** July 24, 2026

Varq is a local macOS e-reader. This policy describes how the app handles information when you use it.

## Information Varq stores

Varq stores the books you import, their metadata, reading positions, highlights, notes, collections, and reading preferences locally in its macOS app sandbox. Imported files are copied into Varq’s managed library so they remain available if the original selected file moves or is deleted.

If you mark a book private, Varq encrypts its managed copy at rest. The encryption key is stored in the system Keychain with the authentication protection provided by macOS. Varq does not receive or store your Touch ID biometric data.

## Information Varq collects or shares

Varq does not require an account. Varq does not include advertising, analytics, tracking, or a remote synchronization service in this release. Varq does not sell or share your library, reading positions, highlights, notes, or private-book data with third parties.

## Your choices

You can delete books, notes, highlights, and collections from within Varq. Deleting a book removes its managed local copy and its associated reading data. You can remove a private designation from a book to decrypt its managed local copy.

## Security

No method of storage is completely secure. Keep your Mac account protected, and use the private-book feature for books that need additional protection at rest.

## Changes to this policy

If Varq introduces an account, analytics, cloud sync, or another feature that changes how information is handled, this policy will be updated before that feature is released.
